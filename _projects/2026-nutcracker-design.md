For my statics class, I was asked to come up with a nutcracker design that could crush a macadamia nut. Since a basic design would just be two members connected by a pin, my task was to find the length of the members. I researched the force it takes to crush a macadamia nut, along witht the average size of one (assuming they are spherical), and I also found the average male grip strength. Using these values, my plan was to use the equation r1F1 = r2F2, where r1 is the distance from the nut to the pivot (pin), F1 is the force needed to crush the nut, r2 is the distance from the pivot to the point where hand force is applied, and F2 is the applied grip strength. By rearranging the equation to solve for the unknown length r2, I determined the necessary handle length to ensure the average person could generate enough mechanical advantage to crack the nut. After inputting my values for nut size, crushing force, and grip strength, I ended up with a required handle length of 1.82 inches from the pivot point.
